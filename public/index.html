openFDA drug labels → knowledge graph → cited answers

Every fact in this graph can show its receipt.

3,000 raw drug-label records from the openFDA API, parsed and entity-resolved into a store-neutral graph — companies, the products they make, the active ingredients those products contain. One pipeline feeds an RDF triple store, Neo4j, and a live question-answering box right under the graph. No edge exists without a source, a confidence score, a date, and the id of the record that asserted it.

The provenance contract

  

    

      
Every relationship carries four fields: the source that asserted it, a
      confidence score from documented rules, the as-of date of the underlying
      record, and the source_record id you could take back to openFDA and verify.

      
The contract is enforced, not aspirational — SHACL shapes validate it when the graph
      loads into RDF, and the answering endpoint below is required to cite these ids rather
      than speak from the model's memory.

      
2,240 edges at confirmed confidence · 1,142 scored lower
      by rule — uncertainty is recorded, not hidden.
